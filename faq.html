What was the inspiration for your character on Everybody Loves Raymond?
The character of Ray Barone was loosely based on myself. I wanted to create a character who was relatable and flawed, someone who was just trying to do his best but often made mistakes. I also wanted to explore the dynamics of family life, which can be both hilarious and heartbreaking at the same time.
What was the biggest challenge you faced while filming Everybody Loves Raymond?
The biggest challenge was probably the time commitment. We were filming 24 episodes a year, which meant that we were working very long hours. It was also difficult to balance my personal life with my work life, especially when my children were young.
What is your favorite thing about being a comedian?
He lovs making people laugh. There's nothing quite like the feeling of getting a big laugh from an audience. It's also a great way to connect with people and share his own experiences.
